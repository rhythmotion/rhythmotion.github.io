MissionMovement through Play

Movement that feels rewarding, not repetitive.

Players follow specific head and arm patterns tracked through the headset and controllers in real time. Instead of feeling like routine exercises, those movements become part of the gameplay, encouraging players to move naturally as they play.

The experience is designed to make intentional movement feel rewarding, not repetitive.

Why RhythMotion?

Most rhythm games train timing. RhythMotion trains movement.

By combining VR motion tracking with movement patterns inspired by physical therapy, RhythMotion transforms repetitive exercises into an engaging rhythm experience.

Gameplay

Two stages. Two ways to move.

Each stage translates a different set of mobility-focused movements into rhythm gameplay, pairing music and visual cues with real-time headset and controller tracking.

NECK MOBILITY

Star Gazing Stage

Focused on neck mobility, Star Gazing challenges players to align with targets and follow moving star paths synchronized with the music. The target paths can mirror movements such as rotation, flexion, and extension while also encouraging natural actions like looking and turning.

Head tracking · Seated play

ARM MOBILITY

Stellar Stretch Stage

Focused on arm and upper-body mobility, Stellar Stretch asks players to reach for rhythm targets and trace ribbon-like paths with their controllers. The movements encourage fluid, controlled motion across different heights, directions, and ranges.

Controller tracking · Standing play

Research & Validation

Shaped by research. Refined through testing.

Across two rounds of user testing, RhythMotion was evaluated by physical therapy specialists, adults with prior rehabilitation experience, and members of the general public. Their feedback guided improvements to gameplay, usability, movement feedback, and onboarding.
